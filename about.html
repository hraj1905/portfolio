What is Soil Pollution?

Soil pollution occurs when the concentration of chemicals, nutrients, or harmful materials in the soil becomes high enough to cause harm to plant life, animals, and humans. These contaminants often result from industrial activities, agricultural pesticides, and improper waste disposal.

Causes of Soil Pollution

Common causes of soil pollution include excessive use of fertilizers and pesticides, deforestation, industrial waste, and the dumping of hazardous waste. These activities alter the natural composition of the soil, making it less fertile and harmful for the ecosystem.

Effects of Soil Pollution

Soil pollution can lead to reduced agricultural productivity, health problems in humans and animals, and the degradation of ecosystems. Contaminated soil can also pollute nearby water sources, creating a domino effect of environmental hazards.

Types of Soil Pollution

Biological Pollution: Caused by microorganisms, parasites, or harmful bacteria.

Chemical Pollution: Resulting from pesticides, fertilizers, and industrial chemicals.

Radioactive Pollution: Contamination by radioactive materials from nuclear waste or accidents.

Solid Waste Pollution: Caused by improper disposal of plastics, metals, and other solid wastes.
